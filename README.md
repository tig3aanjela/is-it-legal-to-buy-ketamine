## Understanding Ketamine: A Brief Overview
Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic with medical applications, such as pain management and treatment for depression. Developed in the 1960s, it's classified as a Schedule III controlled substance under the U.S. Controlled Substances Act, reflecting its potential for abuse alongside accepted medical uses. In 2026, its legal framework continues to evolve due to growing research into therapeutic benefits, but recreational or non-prescribed use remains heavily restricted globally.

### Medical vs. Non-Medical Use
Ketamine is legal when prescribed by a licensed healthcare provider for approved conditions like anesthesia or off-label depression treatment via FDA-approved nasal sprays (e.g., esketamine). Purchasing it without a prescription is illegal in most countries, including the US, UK, Canada, and EU nations. Penalties can include fines, imprisonment, or both, depending on quantity and intent.

In the US, the DEA enforces strict controls, with federal law prohibiting unauthorized possession or distribution. Similar rules apply in the EU under the UN Convention on Psychotropic Substances. Exceptions exist for research or veterinary use, but personal purchases require oversight.
